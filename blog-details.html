Certified Diamonds: KGK Group’s Commitment to Quality
Published July 31, 2024

Significance of Certified Diamonds

It is quite important to look for certifications when you are looking to buy manufactured diamonds. A certification helps the buyers with an unbiased picture about the quality and the characteristics of a diamond. The Gemological Institute of America (GIA) has been the most renowned and credible amongst all global certification bodies.

Why are GIA Certified Diamonds better?

Being regarded as one of the best Here’s why GIA certification is considered the gold standard for wholesale diamonds:

  • Objective and Unbiased: GIA is a non-profit organization with no vested interest in diamond sales.
  • Consistent Grading: GIA’s standardized process ensures reliability across all certified diamonds.
  • Comprehensive Analysis: Each diamond is thoroughly examined for all aspects of the 4Cs.
  • Advanced Technology: GIA uses state-of-the-art equipment for accurate assessments.

The 4Cs of Diamond Quality

For buyers who are looking for high quality diamonds, it is important to understand the 4Cs of a diamond stone.

1. Color

GIA has specified a scale for diamonds which ranges from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown).

2. Clarity

Clarity has a spectrum of Ranges within the terminology being Flawless Diamonds (FL) to Diamonds with obvious inclusions (I3)

3. Cut

GIA grades cut from Excellent to Poor, focusing on how well the diamond interacts with light.

4. Carat Weight

This measures the diamond’s weight, where one carat equals 0.2 grams.

The GIA Certification Process

Each diamond undergoes a meticulous examination:

  1. Initial Screening: Determining if the stone is a natural diamond.
  2. Cleaning: Ensuring an accurate assessment.
  3. Color Grading: Comparing the diamond to master stones.
  4. Clarity Grading: Examining the diamond under 10x magnification.
  5. Cut Grading: Assessing proportions and symmetry.
  6. Carat Weight: Precise measurement to the nearest 0.001 carat.
